An AI Prompt Management Platform is a system designed to organize, store, version, test, and 
evaluate prompts used in AI applications. Instead of treating prompts as simple text, the platform 
manages them as structured assets with metadata such as title, description, author, tags, and 
creation date. This helps teams maintain a centralized repository of prompts, track how prompts 
evolve over time, and quickly search or retrieve them based on business needs. By supporting 
multiple prompt versions, the platform ensures that updates do not overwrite previous work, making it 
easier to compare prompt improvements and maintain a clear history of changes. 
In addition to storage and versioning, the platform provides a testing and evaluation workflow for 
measuring prompt quality. Users can execute prompts with input variables, view AI-generated 
responses, store execution history, and submit feedback based on quality, accuracy, and usefulness. 
Over time, this allows teams to identify which prompt versions perform best and make data-driven 
improvements. In a real-world enterprise setting, such a platform improves consistency, 
collaboration, maintainability, and governance of AI-driven workflows, especially when multiple 
developers, analysts, or business teams are working with prompts across different use cases.
